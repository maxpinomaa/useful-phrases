Quick learning tips

Learning languages is much easier when you follow at least some of the advice of experienced polyglots. It can be quite inspiring to see interviews of people who speak even 10+ languages fluently (and even while having a life!). If they can become fluent in ten or more, you can easily do at least one more, right?

Experienced polyglots have found ways of making the learning process more fun and interesting. More about the bigwig polyglots.

Learning a language is usually a long road, but with the right advice not necessarily as long as most people think. Learning conversational connectors and common questions can be seen as shortcuts, or at least boosts, in the road to fluency.

There are many fun and often free resources such as the website LingQ (and it's smartphone app) with its huge library of interesting interviews and texts with audio. I also warmly recommend the Simply Learn apps.

Comics are also great because they often contain both formal and informal speech. At least for me, reading fun (or serious) comics does not feel like studying, even though I sometimes use the dictionary and write down some of the sentences.
